Course Objectives:

 

At completion of course, student will be able to

·         Build and test electrical circuits; correlate experimental results with analytical expectations

·         Design, build, and test instrumentation and control systems; compare experimental results with analytical expectations.

·         Perform basic circuit analysis using computer-based tools such as PSPICE.

·         ABET outcomes addressed:

A: Ability to apply knowledge of mathematics, science and engineering.

B: Ability to design and conduct experiments as well as analyze and interpret data.

K: Ability to use techniques, skills and modern engineering tools necessary for engineering practices.

Laboratory Policies:

 

General: Lab assignments are intended to be performed outside the lab prior to the student’s scheduled lab time (see above).  Scheduled lab times are intended to be used for demonstrating your circuits’ performance to the TAs.  TAs can help you with questions relative to the lab and your circuits’ performance, but you must have made a legitimate attempt to do the lab on your own prior to your lab time.  TAs can refuse to assist you if it becomes apparent that you have expended little or no effort to perform the lab assignment prior to your lab time.  No food or drink is allowed in the lab.

 

Students are to perform the labs individually.  Consultation with other students is permitted, but students must submit their own work.  Lab assignments will generally include demonstrations of your circuits’ operation to a lab assistant; every student will be expected to answer questions about the circuit’s operation during demonstrations.  Apparent unfamiliarity with the circuits’ operation will result in deduction of points.  All lab worksheets must be individual efforts.

 

Lab Documents: Lab assignments are provided for each lab on the web site http://personal.palouse.net/thanshaw/ee261.

 

Lab Assignment Submission and Grading Policy:  Lab assignments are to be done prior to the lab session, at the student’s convenience.  Each lab assignment on the above-listed website is accompanied by a lab worksheet.  The lab worksheets provide a list of lab results to be submitted for grading, and the weight of each result.  Completed lab worksheets are to be submitted to the TA for grading per the schedule provided on the class web site.  Lab worksheets must contain TA initials, indicating that the required demonstrations have been performed; no credit will be provided for a submitted worksheet not including initials.
