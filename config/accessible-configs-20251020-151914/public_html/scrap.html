Category Descriptions

Ferrous

The term prepared means cut to a size less than 5' long and 3' wide. (4' x 2' Kitsap)
  • #1 prepared iron: heavy melting steel (HMS) or iron thicker than 1/4" and less than 4-5' long x 2-3' wide (4' max Kitsap)
  • #1 unprepared iron: heavy melting steel (HMS) or wrought iron thicker than 1/4"
  • #2 iron prepared: any iron over 1/8" thick and less than 5' long x 3' wide (4' max Kitsap)
  • #2 iron unprepared: any iron over 1/8" thick
  • Shred: steel thinner than 1/4" thick e.g. mop handles, bike frames, air ducts

Non-Ferrous

  • Bare Bright: solid, no thinner than 16 AWG. No tarnishing or patina. No stranded at Kitsap. Any bare stranded is #2 Copper at Kitsap.
  • #1 Copper: bus bars, clippings, commutator segments, and wire of at least 1/16" diameter (16 awg). Free of paint, fittings, insulation, and solder.
  • #2 Copper: bare wire thinner than 16 awg (any stranded), pipe or solid metal (ferrules), solder, paint, coating present.
  • #2 Insulated Wire: thinner than 16 awg. Communications wire, etc.
  • #1 Insulated Wire: 16 awg or larger, clean w/ all ends cut off.
