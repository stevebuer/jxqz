Exam Format:

Exams will be closed book, closed notes. "Crib" sheets will be allowed (standard 8 1/2" by 11" or smaller paper only, please).  One crib sheet will be allowed on the first exam, two on the second, and three on the third. The final exam will be comprehensive; students may bring in up to four crib sheets for the final.

 

Attendance and Assignment submission:

·         Attendance in lecture is expected but does not contribute to the students’ grade.  Students are responsible for any information provided during lecture.

·         Late homework and lab assignments are not allowed.  The lowest homework score is dropped.  No make-up homework or lab assignments are allowed.

·         Make-up exams are not allowed. 

 

Excused Absences:

For all but medical emergencies, excused absences must be approved in a face-to-face office visit with instructor before the missed lecture or project review. In the case of medical emergencies, the absence remains unexcused until a face-to-face office visit with instructor.

 

Reasonable accommodation will be made for excused absences.  Students are encouraged to submit assignments they will miss as a result of an excused absence early if at all possible.  Missed homework or laboratory assignments resulting from an excused absence will be dropped from the students’ grade.  A missed exam due to an excused absence will be replaced with the final exam score.

 

 

Academic Integrity:

Academic integrity is the cornerstone of higher education. As such, all members of the university community share responsibility for maintaining and promoting the principles of integrity in all activities, including academic integrity and honest scholarship. Academic integrity will be strongly enforced in this course. Students who violate WSU’s Academic Integrity Policy (identified in Washington Administrative Code (WAC) 504-26-010(3) and -404) will receive results in a grade of “F” for the course, will not have the option to withdraw from the course pending an appeal, and will be reported to the Office of Student Conduct.

 

Cheating includes, but is not limited to, plagiarism and unauthorized collaboration as defined in the Standards of Conduct for Students, WAC 504-26-010(3). You need to read and understand all of the definitions of cheating: http://app.leg.wa.gov/WAC/default.aspx?cite=504-26-010.  If you have any questions about what is and is not allowed in this course, you should ask course instructors before proceeding.

 

If you wish to appeal a faculty member's decision relating to academic integrity, please use the form available at conduct.wsu.edu..

 

Disability accommodation:
